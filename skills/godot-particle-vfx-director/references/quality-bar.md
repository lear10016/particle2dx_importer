# Quality Bar

AAA-like is the target bar, not a promise from a single pass. Reach for clarity, layering, timing, and polish that would not look out of place beside a strong production effect.

## Non-negotiables

- Make the effect readable from the actual gameplay camera.
- Give the effect a dominant shape, a secondary shape, and a breakup layer.
- Separate fast bright energy from slower soft aftermath.
- Use timing phases instead of one flat emission block.
- Reuse textures intentionally rather than filling space with count alone.

## Layering standard

For an important effect, expect at least:

- one core or focal layer
- one directional or burst layer
- one secondary breakup layer
- one tail or residue layer

Add ring, floor contact, haze, or sparkle accents only after the base read works.

## Timing standard

Look for a clear arc:

- anticipation or charge
- peak frame
- release
- tail

If every layer starts and ends together, the effect will feel cheap even if the textures are good.

## Shape standard

- Use one clean silhouette that can be understood in a glance.
- Support it with one different shape family, such as streak plus ring, orb plus sparks, or slash plus mist.
- Preserve negative space. If the whole frame is full, nothing reads as special.

## Color standard

- Choose one dominant hue family and one accent family.
- Keep the brightest values concentrated at the focal point.
- Desaturate smoke, fog, and residue unless the brief explicitly calls for stylized color.
- Use additive on the hottest layers only; too much additive turns everything to flat white.

## Motion standard

- Mix at least two motion behaviors, such as directional travel plus radial breakup, or orbit plus vertical drift.
- Avoid identical speeds, scales, and angles across all layers.
- Prefer slight asymmetry and stagger over perfect symmetry unless the brief calls for ritual or UI precision.

## Common failure patterns

- one giant emitter doing every job
- large opaque smoke covering the focal point
- beautiful still frame but dead tail
- too many particles with no value hierarchy
- every layer sharing the same lifetime and start time
- overusing star textures with no soft support layer

## Review checklist

- Can you identify the focal point in less than a second?
- Does the effect still read when paused at peak?
- Does the tail feel intentional instead of leftover spam?
- Would reducing particle count by 20 percent actually improve clarity?
- Are at least two local references visible in the final design language?
- If this is a hero effect, does it feel authored rather than copied from one sample?
